The Mix: A natural or man-made alloy of gold and silver (usually at least 20% silver).

BUT WHY IN THE NAME OF HADES AND HIS GOLD IS GOLD GOLDEN?
April 29, 2026
It comes down to a battle between Newton’s physics and Einstein’s relativity.
In most metals (like silver), the electrons are "slow" and "light." In gold, things get weird because gold atoms are massive.
1. The "Relativistic" Squeeze
Gold has 79 protons. That huge positive charge pulls its innermost electrons toward the nucleus at half the speed of light.
Because they are moving so fast, Einstein’s Relativity kicks in.
The electrons actually gain mass.
This extra mass makes the electron orbits shrink.
2. The Energy Gap (The "Blue Trap")
When those orbits shrink, the energy levels of the outer electrons change. Specifically, the "gap" between energy bands (the 
 and 
 bands) gets smaller.
In Silver: This gap is huge. It takes high-energy Ultraviolet light to jump the gap. Since it doesn't absorb visible light, it reflects everything and looks like a mirror (white).
In Gold: Because of that relativistic shrinking, the gap is much smaller. It is exactly the right size to absorb Blue and Violet light.
3. The Resulting Glow
Because gold "eats" the blue and violet part of the white light hitting it, the only colors left to bounce back to your eyes are Red and Yellow.
If Einstein’s theory of relativity didn't exist, gold would look exactly like silver.
